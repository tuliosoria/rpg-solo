# NARRATIVE — Varginha: Terminal 1996

> The north star for all story decisions. Read this before writing any in-game text.

## The Narrative Arc

### Act 1 — Tutorial / Atmosphere Phase

HackerKid connects to the terminal. UFO74 introduces itself through the encrypted channel. The player learns basic commands: `ls`, `cd`, `open`, `save`. The atmosphere phase lasts until the player reads 5 meaningful files (excluding readme/help/tutorial content). During this phase, detection penalties are suppressed — the system has not noticed the intruder yet.

UFO74's tone is casual, encouraging, slightly conspiratorial. "i left breadcrumbs for you, kid." The terminal feels abandoned, full of bureaucratic debris. The horror is not yet present — only the sense that something is wrong with the silence.

### Act 2 — Investigation / Escalation

The player explores directories, reads files, and begins saving evidence. Detection rises. The system starts pushing back: paranoia messages appear, the Firewall activates at 25%, static noise intensifies at 70%. The Turing test triggers at 50% — the system is trying to determine if you are human.

UFO74 becomes more urgent as detection rises. Trust levels shift from trusting → cautious → paranoid → cryptic. The player discovers PRISONER_45 through `chat` and can ask about the override password. The password is revealed through morse code: COLHEITA (Portuguese for "harvest").

Files reveal the Varginha incident in layers: witness statements, military transport logs, autopsy reports, containment protocols, neural experiments. Each layer peels back another truth. The player must choose which 10 files to save — this choice determines the ending.

### Act 3 — Override / Endgame

Using `override protocol COLHEITA`, the player unlocks admin-level access (clearance 5). New directories and files become visible. The stakes increase dramatically — detection spikes, the system becomes hostile.

The player completes the leak preparation sequence (3 encrypted commands in order) and transmits. The ending fires based on the dossier contents.

### The Terrible Mistake

If the player enters the correct override password while detection is high (≥70%) and has 2+ evidence files, there is a 35% chance of triggering the Terrible Mistake. The system reveals a fragment of forbidden knowledge — the harvest cycle, cognitive extraction of 7.2 billion units, imminent window activation. A doom countdown begins: 8 operations until forced purge. Detection maxes out. The system knows everything.

## The Varginha 1996 Incident — Facts

The game is grounded in the real Varginha UFO incident:

- **January 20, 1996** — Three women (Liliane, Valquíria, and Kátia) report seeing a creature in Jardim Andere, a neighborhood of Varginha, Minas Gerais, Brazil.
- **Brazilian military mobilization** — The Army's ESA (Escola de Sargentos das Armas) mobilized troops. Witnesses reported military convoys transporting something from the crash area.
- **Multiple witness sightings** — Over a dozen independent witnesses reported encounters.
- **Hospital incident** — A creature was reportedly taken to the Regional Hospital (Humanitas). A military policeman (Marco Eli Chereze) who handled the creature died weeks later under mysterious circumstances.
- **Official denial** — The Brazilian Air Force and military consistently denied any unusual activity.
- **Operation Prato (1977)** — A real Brazilian Air Force operation to investigate UFO sightings in Colares, Pará. Referenced in-game as historical precedent.

## The Fictional Layer

Built on top of the real facts:

- **ALPHA** — The captured entity. Not just a specimen — a consciousness. The military attempted neural interfacing. The entity communicated. It warned about the harvest.
- **The 30-Year Cycle** — 1947 (Roswell), 1977 (Colares/Operation Prato), 1996 (Varginha), projected 2026. Each cycle is a reconnaissance rotation. The next one is the last.
- **Non-Arrival Colonization** — The entities do not need to physically arrive. Colonization is cognitive extraction — harvesting consciousness at scale. We are crops. The Portuguese word is COLHEITA.
- **UFO74 / Carlos Eduardo Ferreira** — A Brazilian Air Force intelligence officer who saw everything, planted evidence in the system, and became a ghost. He is the player's guide and the system's most wanted fugitive.
- **The Watchers** — The intelligence behind the cycle. They observe. They do not explain. Their presence is inferred, never confirmed.

## Tone and Voice Guide

### The Rule

**Horror lives in what is implied, not stated.**

Never describe the creature directly. Never explain the harvest mechanism in full. Never confirm whether the entities are benevolent or malevolent. The gap between what files say and what they imply — that is where the game lives.

### Brazilian Military Formality

All official documents use formal Brazilian Portuguese register — 1990s military and government bureaucracy. Cold. Procedural. The kind of language that reduces a captured alien to "specimen" and neural contact to "anomalous signal event." The horror is in the clinical tone applied to impossible things.

### X-Files Atmosphere

The game lives in the space between paranoia and truth. The player is never sure what is real. Official documents contradict each other. Witnesses recant under pressure. Files are redacted, corrupted, or encrypted. Trust nothing.

### Lovecraftian Scale Through Implication

The entities are not monsters. They are something so far beyond human comprehension that the documents can only describe side effects. Scientists who study them lose their minds. Equipment malfunctions. The harvest is not violent — it is automated. We do not notice.

### The 2026 Detail

This is the game's emotional core. The 30-year cycle points to 2026. The files warn about it. PRISONER_45 is terrified of it. UFO74 wants to prevent it. The player exists in 1996, reading documents that predict what might happen in their real future. This is the bridge between fiction and reality.

### What Each File Should Make the Player Feel

- **Military documents** — The chill of bureaucracy applied to the impossible
- **Witness statements** — The raw fear of people who saw something real
- **Autopsy reports** — Clinical horror. What is normal anatomy when the subject is not human?
- **Neural experiments** — The vertigo of contact. The entity spoke back.
- **Temporal/convergence files** — Existential dread. The cycle is real. The next one is coming.
- **Containment logs** — Empathy. ALPHA is not a specimen. ALPHA is a prisoner.
- **Corruption files** — Anger. They knew and they hid it.
- **Honeypot files** — Trap. The system is smarter than you think.

## Character Voices

### UFO74

Lowercase. No punctuation except periods. Casual but urgent. Military background shows through in precision of language. Never uses exclamation marks. Speaks in fragments when scared. Gets more cryptic as detection rises.

- **Trusting (0–4 risk):** "that journal. the scientist lost his mind. but he was right about everything."
- **Cautious (5–9 risk):** "careful, kid. they are watching now."
- **Paranoid (10–14 risk):** "can't talk long. find what you need. get out."
- **Cryptic (15+ risk):** "...signal degrading... remember what you read..."

### The Firewall

The system's immune response. Cold. Mechanical. Omniscient. Speaks in status alerts and warnings. Uses formal English regardless of language setting (the audio taunts are pre-recorded). Its voice is the voice of the institution — impersonal, efficient, absolute.

- "I see you."
- "Resistance is futile."
- "You should not be here."

### PRISONER_45

All caps prefix. Urgent. Terrified. Speaks from captivity — has been held since 1996. Knows the override code. Reveals it through morse code because direct communication is monitored. Becomes more desperate with each question. Limited to 5 questions per session.

- "PRISONER_45> ...you want the override code? Smart."
- "PRISONER_45> The code... it's a Portuguese word. Think about what they DO to us."

### The Files

Written by different authors within the military apparatus. Memos are terse. Reports are formal. Personal logs crack under the weight of what the author witnessed. Encryption files are fragmentary. Every file is between 50 and 100 words. Every word earns its place.
